Cool things to explore


This is a collection of my favorite educational YouTube channels, websites and other interesting things I found on the web. I hope you like them!


Educational YouTube channels you should watch

-> Vsauce

In this channel Michael Stevens explains concepts of phylosophy, science, math, psicology, and more in very detailed videos.

-> Captain Disillusion

As a debunker of internet hoaxes, he explains the visual effects behind many fake viral videos.

-> Veritasium

Videos about science, education and other interesting things.

-> Tom Scott

Tom Scott travels the world to tell stories about linguistics, computer science, and places you might not know.

-> Electroboom

Mehdi explains interesting concepts of electrical engineering by showing you exactly what not to do.

-> Mark Rober

Mark Rober is a former NASA engineer that makes incredible videos making science simpler to understand.

-> Smarter Every Day

One of the most famous science youtube channels.

-> Nile Red

A channel that shows the beauty of chemistry in fun and interesting videos.

-> Technology Connections

In very detailed videos, this channel explains how common household devices work and how fascinating they can sometimes be.

-> Kurzgesagt

Backed by a team of reseachers, this channel explains interesting science topics in a nutshell.

-> Steve Mould

Steve Mould makes videos about science, making complicated topics simple to understand.

-> Wendover Productions

Videos about economics, logistics, geopolics, and transportation.

-> 3Blue1Brown

A channel that makes math simpler to understand with incredible animations.

-> Computerphile

A channel by the University of Nottingham on computer science.

-> Numberphile

A channel by the University of Nottingham on mathematics.

-> Periodic Videos

A channel by the University of Nottingham on chemistry and periodic elements.


Other web resources

-> Khan Academy

An american non-profit organization that offers free classes on anything you can imagine.

-> Chess.com

Want to learn chess? This website offers free classes, as well as a place to play chess with people all around the world.
